Study of Wine

Here I am with some 100 year old (!!!) vines in the Barossa Valley, Australia.

I got started with wine in 2015 when I arrived in Melbourne, Australia for my first academic job. Oz has a proper wine culture and I was like a barbarian at the gates. I took to it quickly, though, and even contributed to overhauling the wine cellar at Ormond College during my tenure as President of the Senior Common Room.

Read more

Learning about wine is both theoretical and experiential. It is also Socratic. Being in conversation with other wine lovers is the greatest strategy for increasing understanding about wine. I have my WSET Level 3, and I have begun my Diploma work with Peter Neptune MS. I have shadowed pickers, pruners, winemakers, and sommeliers. I even get to tend the vines that my dad planted on his farm in Texas.

Research on Wine

These are the fermentation eggs at Yangarra Estate in McLaren Vale, Australia.

My research work on wine has to wait for post-tenure. I am generally interested in vineyard management and fermentation (specifically, the way that winemakers use yeast), and I'm curious about ancient practice along these two dimensions.
